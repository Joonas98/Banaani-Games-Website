SAM PROJECT

2022 - UI / UX Design and Implementation

SAM is short for Smart Autonomous Mobility. SAM is a big project from Metropolia University of Applied sciences along with some other similar schools.

SAM aims to innovate and develop vehicles to level 4 or even level 5 of autonomous driving. To simplify, this means there will be no driving wheel or pedals and driver does not need to do anything.

I made my engineer's thesis for this project and I was hired to continue afterwards. For the most part, I worked on the UI / UX for an application that test how many autonomous vehicles one supervisor can supervise. In addition my UI design could work as a base for a real supervising application, as there aren't similar designs openly available.

Nords and Swords

2020 - Lead Programmer

Nords and Swords was a game project with programmers / developers from Metropolia University of Applied Sciences and artists from Stadin AO Vocational College.

The game was inspired from flash game series called Swords and Sandals. The player fights opponents in turn based combat. Fighting grants the player experience and money to upgrade their attributes and equipment

I was the lead programmer. I ensured that everything gets developed by someone, and I picked up "leftovers" myself, such as menus, scene handling, camera movement, audio implementation etc.

Balls and Boxes

2019 - Solo Project

Balls and Boxes is a simple brick breaker game. It was a short (under 2 weeks) school project that I developed alone.

The ball gets faster with time to make it far more interesting. Later levels have different brick types like some require more hits or some consist of multiple smaller bricks. There are also few basic powerups.

Sound effects and few tricks improve the gameplay, making it surprisingly fun. By few tricks I mean e.g. the player's plane has tuned colliders, so you can control the ball better. Left and right walls are slightly slanted downwards, so the ball doesn't take such a long time to return to player.

Grainraiders

2019 - First proper game project

My first proper game project. It was made during my first year of school with two other students. Our class voted Grainraiders to be the best out of ten games.

The story and gameplay is very simple. You defend your village's food with a revolver and dynamite bundles. Every five waves, you get a short break to loot. Money is used to refill ammo and dynamites. You lose the game when the bandits are able to steal 10 sacks of food.

The gameplay is quite difficult but fun. After few waves, there will be so many enemies, that it's basically all about timing your dynamites well and finishing survivors with the revolver.
